# Awesome AI-driven plant biology

This repository provides a curated collection of research and review papers on **AI-based methods in plant science**. It will be continuously updated to track advances at the frontier of AI-driven plant biology. Feel free to follow and star the project.

## Background

Plant science is entering an era of data-driven discovery — from phenomenology to programmability. The field is evolving too rapidly with new architectures, foundation models, and cross-disciplinary advances are emerging at an unprecedented pace. To address this, we created this repository as a living, community-driven knowledge hub for AI in plant biology. It continuously curates and organizes machine learning advances relevant to plant research, including methods developed beyond the plant domain but adaptable to it.

To the best of our knowledge, this is the first open and systematically organized collection of AI research dedicated to plant science. Papers are categorized by biological application and model type to enable structured exploration and interdisciplinary connection. Our vision is to provide an evolving resource that helps researchers navigate, compare, and apply AI approaches in plant biology, fostering transparency, accessibility, and a more connected research ecosystem.

We welcome contributions from the community. If you would like to suggest new papers or improve the taxonomy, please open an issue or submit a pull request. Together, we can build an up-to-date resource that evolves alongside the field.

Zhai J, Gokaslan A, Hsu SK, et al.  *bioRxiv*, Dec, 2025. https://doi.org/10.1101/2025.08.27.672609

- a new version of PlantCaduceus (PlantCAD). This new version trained on 65 plant genomes and increased context DNA length from 512 to 8192bp. Notably, the model is reverse-complement equivariant.
- Fine-tuning this model on downstream tasks like accessibility, gene expression, TFBS prediction also demonstrated SOTA. The performance surpassed ArgoNT, GPN (trained on A.th.) and a general model Evo2 (7B).
- Genuinely, there are not many plant gLMs. I wonder whether the zero-shot performance of PlantCAD2 is better than task-specific foundation models like sequence-to-accessibility models or sequence-to-expression models. Cell-type-specificity and tissue specificity of epigenetic marks and expression are also modeled.
- I would love to see whether the PlantCAD2 can be applied to the CRE design or in silico evolution of gene function/expresion.
